In this case, we are interested in correlation not in causality. This is so because, for a given car and driver, we want to be able to detect counterband, not to address its causes in order to prevent it from happening. For example, if male drivers have a higher rate of counterband activity than female drivers, it doesn't necessarily mean (and probably doesn't!) that gender is causing counterband. Nevertheless our algorithm should place a higher (weight) risk on male drivers because it will be more likely to find counterband in the car if the driver is a man.
